Executive Summary
Apex Capital Partners holds a 10 BTC position acquired at an average cost of approximately $72,000/BTC, representing an unrealised gain of ~$232,400 at current spot. This covered call strategy generates $28,410 in upfront premium by selling the right for a counterparty to purchase 10 BTC at $100,000 on or before April 20, 2026.
The strategy captures a 14-point implied volatility premium (72% IV vs 58% 30d realized). At current pricing, the annualised yield on notional is approximately 36.2% — considerably above typical fixed income alternatives.
The position is suitable for clients who are comfortable capping upside above $100,000 over a 30-day period and prioritise income generation over maximum capital appreciation.
Key Risk Considerations
Capped upside. If BTC exceeds $100k at expiry, gains above that level are forfeited. The client would miss a potential $25,760+/BTC rally.
Spot downside risk. The premium ($2,841/BTC) only partially offsets a spot price decline. Full downside below breakeven ($92,400) is borne by the client.
Early assignment risk. American-style options may be assigned early. Client must hold 10 BTC in custody to fulfil delivery obligations.
Volatility spike risk. A sharp IV increase raises the mark-to-market cost of unwinding before expiry. Mid-term exit may result in a loss on the option leg.
